The Wastelands theme is low-tech. Period. The ruins of civilization are all around, but the understanding of twentieth and twenty-first century technology has faded away as those who knew how to make 'tech' died.

Almost two centuries have passed since the war, and few people see the Old World as more than legend. Livestock and farming are more immediate concerns, and a good horse is more valuable than fanciful tales of things called 'cars.' With no power plants, electrical devices are mostly useless. Characters are *certainly* not going to be running around with laser weapons and night-vision goggles. For that matter, even guns, motors, and other complex machinery are extremely rare.

Mechanical devices, like pulleys, winches, locks, crossbows for hunting, etc, are more common. Any machines that require mass-produced parts are rare and treated with great care; guns can be found, but bullets are almost unheard of. Those with mechanical aptitude and creative minds are rare but invaluable to a tribe -- they can often assist in repairing broken machines, or constructing new ones from scavenged parts.

'Found' technology is relatively common, but not all of it is understood. An old solar calculator, grimy and battered from use, is a likely candidate. Unfortunately, only a handful would understand its usefulness -- the calculator's operation would be quite mysterious, since literacy is almost nonexistant. A mountain bike, found in the ruins of an old warehouse, might be one man's most valuable posession. Repairs would be time consuming and fussy, with no replacement parts available. A Bic lighter would be an amazingly useful find. Even after running out of lighter fluid, its flint would be useful for starting tinder.

In general, if your character owns or discovers a piece of old tech, it's cool. Don't go overboard, and drop a line to one of the player staff or a game admin if you have questions about it. [see 'guidelines' for more information]
